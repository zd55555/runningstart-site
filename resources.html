Resources

A collection of tools, courses, and reports I've found useful. This started as a handout for conference presentations and has grown from there. Nothing comprehensive. Just things worth trying.

Try

Tools to get your hands on.

  • Chatbots Perplexity.ai, ChatGPT, Gemini, Claude. Try the different modes: voice, canvas, reasoning, deep research. Use them for hobbies, vacation planning, things you know well and things you don't. For anything high-stakes, check sources.
  • NotebookLM Google's research tool. Try the mindmap, audio overview, and briefing features.
  • MagicSchool.ai Dozens of tools for educators. Good onboarding resources on the site and YouTube.
  • Scispace Academic research assistant. Useful for parsing papers.

Learn

Free courses and webinars.

  • Training Magazine Network Free live webinars and recordings.
  • Generative AI for Educators Google in partnership with MIT.
  • OpenAI Academy Workshops, tutorials, live sessions.
  • Deeplearning.ai More technical. High-quality eLearning modules.
  • AI for Beginners Microsoft's open curriculum.

Read

Blogs, newsletters, and books.

  • One Useful Thing Ethan Mollick's newsletter. Also his book: Co-Intelligence: Living & Working with AI.
  • AI x Education Newsletter covering AI in learning contexts.

Listen

Podcasts.

  • Lex Fridman Podcast Long conversations with thought leaders.
  • AI Breakdown Daily AI news roundup.

Teach

Resources for educators.

  • MIT Sloan Generative AI Resource Hub
  • Code.org AI 101 Video tutorials and classroom activities.
  • AI Pedagogy Project Harvard metaLAB. Assignment collection for integrating ChatGPT, DALL-E, Midjourney.
  • WorldEd AI Literacy for Everyone Includes a list of AI-powered tools for teachers.
  • Syllabi Policies 100+ college syllabus policies for generative AI use.

Research

Reports and data.

  • Stanford AI Index 2025 Tracks and visualizes AI data annually.
  • McKinsey Future of Work Widely cited reports. Good data visualization.
  • Jobs for the Future Multiple reports on AI's workforce impact.
  • Anthropic Education Study Analysis of ~1 million college students' AI usage.
  • IMF Gen-AI and the Future of Work January 2024.
